# Personal Statement Outline

## Your time before ArtCenter

- **Where did you grow up?**
  - Beijing, China

- **What did you enjoy doing?**
  - I love making my ideas real. For me, creating things is a way to connect with people and solve problems together.

- **What was your first meaningful experience with something you would consider to be designed?** (e.g., a place, physical product, or an app)
  - In elementary school, I was excluded from a popular game due to unfair rules. I created a better alternative with fairer rules and more creative possibilities. The new game soon became popular among the class. This experience taught me to "create things I want with my own hands" and it showed me how a design helps people grow.

- **How did these experiences lead you to ArtCenter?**
  - ArtCenter's focus on practice and building prototypes fit perfectly with my belief in solving problems by creating things. I came here to learn how to turn a good idea into a real product that can help more people.

---

## Your time at ArtCenter

- **What have you loved doing during your time at ArtCenter?**
  - At ArtCenter, I was excited to explore how technology can empower people. In one project, I explored how to improve online communication by helping people understand conversations, organize their replies, and become better speakers.

- **What about design excites you?**
  - I enjoy user research and testing because I know my design can only be helpful if I truly understand the users.

---

## Your time outside ArtCenter

- **What are your hobbies?**
  - Making games, drawing, and working on design projects that help others.

- **What makes you happy when you’re away from the computer?**
  - Drawing and writing to capture and manage ideas. Running, observing nature, cooking, and spending time with my family.

---

## Beyond ArtCenter

- **What do you hope to be doing after ArtCenter?**
  - I hope to join a team that builds tools or platforms. My ideal job is to design a system that helps people reach their full potential.

- **What would be your ideal team to work with?** (e.g., vibe, makeup, skills)
  - Open, supportive, and purpose-driven. It's a diverse and collaborative group where we are encouraged to think deeply and grow together.

- **What would give you the feeling of satisfaction in your work?**
  - Knowing that my work is valuable to users and helps them solve their own problems.

- **What would you not want to be doing?**
  - Work that is inefficient, offers no chances to learn, or creates no real value for society.
